Yes — this is a strong app concept.

I’m assuming you meant **“scenarios,”** not “soneros.” A **sonero** is actually a salsa/son singer, so “negative possible scenarios” seems like the intended phrase here. ([yourdictionary.com][1])

What you’re describing feels like a mix of:

- morning mental training,
- real-time emotional coaching,
- and a 30-day Stoic-style diary.

A good version of the app could work like this:

## Core idea

Every morning, the app gives you **one possible difficult scenario**:

- “Someone ignores your text.”
- “You make a mistake in public.”
- “Plans change at the last minute.”
- “Someone criticizes your work.”

Then the app guides you through a response system:

**Step 1: Facts vs story**
“What actually happened?”
“What story am I telling myself about it?”

**Step 2: Control filter**
“What part is in my control?”
“What part is not?”

**Step 3: Choose response**
“What meaning do I want to give this?”
“What is the strongest next action?”

**Step 4: Diary**
Save a short entry:

- scenario
- what got triggered
- what you felt
- how you responded
- what you learned

## Best app flow

### Morning mode

The user opens the app and gets:

- 1 negative scenario
- a 30-second reflection
- a short “pre-rehearsal” exercise

Example:

> Scenario: Someone leaves you on read.
> What are the facts?
> What story are you adding?
> Can you control their response?
> What response would make you stronger?

### Real-life trigger mode

During the day, the user types what happened:

> “My friend canceled and now I feel rejected.”

The app detects which steps should fire:

- **Step 1 trigger** if the entry includes assumptions, mind-reading, or emotional storytelling
  Example: “They hate me.”
- **Step 2 trigger** if the entry focuses on things outside the user’s control
  Example: “Why did they do that?”
- **Step 3 trigger** if the user is stuck in reaction and needs a next move
  Example: “What should I do now?”

Then it replies with a guided prompt instead of just storing text.

## Month-long structure

### Week 1: Awareness

Goal: notice reactions.

- Learn facts vs story
- Write one diary entry a day
- Morning scenario every day

### Week 2: Control

Goal: stop wasting energy on what cannot be controlled.

- Tag every entry as controllable / uncontrollable
- Build a “control score”

### Week 3: Reframing

Goal: choose meaning on purpose.

- Turn setbacks into neutral or useful interpretations
- Practice better self-talk

### Week 4: Resilience

Goal: become calmer and faster.

- Shorter prompts
- More real-world logging
- End-of-week reflection on progress

## Simple MVP

For version 1, I would keep it very simple:

**Home**

- today’s scenario
- start reflection button

**Log**

- text box: “What happened?”
- app analyzes and triggers Step 1, 2, or 3

**Diary**

- daily entries
- mood + trigger tags
- lessons learned

**Progress**

- streak
- number of entries
- which step gets triggered most
- weekly summary

## Smart trigger logic

You do not need complicated AI at first.

You can start with rules like:

**Step 1 keywords**

- always, never, everyone, nobody
- “they think…”
- “I’m worthless”
- “this means…”

**Step 2 keywords**

- why did they
- it’s not fair
- they should
- I can’t make them

**Step 3 keywords**

- what do I do
- now what
- I want to react
- I feel like texting back / quitting / exploding

Then later you can make it smarter with AI.

## Why this idea is good

It is not just a quotes app.
It gives people a daily practice **and** a system for real moments when emotions hit. That makes it much more useful.

The key is this:
do **not** make the scenarios too dark or catastrophic.
Make them realistic and everyday:

- awkwardness
- rejection
- delays
- criticism
- uncertainty

That keeps it practical instead of overwhelming.
